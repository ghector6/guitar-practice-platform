# Guitar Practice Analytics Platform

A domain-driven platform designed to measure, organize, and analyze guitar practice using principles from Business Intelligence, Data Analytics, and Software Engineering.

The project aims to transform musical practice into measurable insights by combining structured study plans, practice tracking, and performance analytics for self-managed musicians, teachers, and educational institutions.

---

## Vision

Traditional music practice is often subjective and difficult to quantify. This project applies the same analytical rigor used in business environments to the development of musical skills.

The platform seeks to answer questions such as:

- How effective is a student's practice?
- Which exercises produce the greatest improvement?
- What technical difficulties are limiting progress?
- When is it appropriate to increase tempo?
- Which students or teaching strategies generate the best results?
- How much repertoire is ready for a concert or examination?

---

## Main Objectives

### Practice Measurement

Capture and organize practice sessions into structured and analyzable data.

### Progress Tracking

Monitor:

- Tempo development
- Practice quality
- Error rates
- Repetition counts
- Technical growth
- Repertoire readiness

### Goal Management

Support short-term and long-term goals:

- Weekly goals
- Monthly goals
- Semester goals
- Concert preparation
- Competitions
- Auditions
- Examinations

### Business Intelligence

Provide meaningful insights that help identify:

- Bottlenecks
- Progress trends
- Effective exercises
- Student engagement
- Teacher effectiveness

### Scalability

Design a domain model capable of evolving from a personal project into a multi-user platform for teachers, students, and institutions.

---

## Target Users

### Self-Managed Musicians

Musicians who want to organize and analyze their own practice.

### Teachers

Professionals responsible for assigning material, defining goals, and monitoring student progress.

### Students

Users who execute study plans and record practice sessions.

### Institutions

Music schools, conservatories, academies, and universities interested in measuring educational outcomes.

---

## Core Domains

### Master Data

Static information that defines the educational environment.

Examples:

- Exercises
- Pieces
- Sections
- Variants
- Technique Elements

---

### Study Plan

Material assigned by teachers or self-managed musicians.

Includes:

- Exercises
- Repertoire sections
- Variants
- Goals

---

### Practice Data

Historical records generated by practice sessions.

Includes:

- Technical practice
- Repertoire practice
- Tempo
- Errors
- Quality metrics

---

### Analytics

Transformation of practice records into actionable insights.

Examples:

- Progress dashboards
- Practice quality indicators
- Goal completion
- Tempo evolution
- Repertoire readiness
