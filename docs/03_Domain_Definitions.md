# Domain Definitions

## Session

A practice session is a continuous interval of time dedicated to studying specific material.

A session may be:

* Technique
* Repertoire
* Mixed

Recommended duration:

20 to 50 minutes.

A session may contain multiple exercises and multiple repertoire sections.

---

## Exercise

Technical material intended to develop one or more technical skills.

Exercises may originate from different methods and sources.

Exercise difficulty is context-dependent and is defined by the teacher.

---

## Variant

A specific way of performing an exercise or a repertoire section.

Variants may include:

* Rhythmic patterns.
* Custom rhythmic combinations.
* Teacher-defined configurations.
* Personalized study approaches.

There is no universal catalog of variants.

---

## Piece

A musical work assigned for study.

A piece consists of one or more sections.

---

## Section

A range of measures belonging to a piece.

Sections isolate specific problems and connect repertoire difficulties with technical exercises.

A section may contain:

* Target tempo.
* Target quality.
* Technical difficulties.

---

## Technique Element

A technical ability that can be developed through exercises and required by repertoire sections.

Examples include:

* Arpeggios
* Scales
* Slurs
* Independence
* Position shifts
* Barre
* Tremolo
* Synchronization

Relationships between exercises and technique elements are many-to-many.

Relationships between sections and technique elements are also many-to-many.

---

## Goal

A measurable objective associated with a student or study plan.

Goals may be:

* Weekly
* Monthly
* Semester-based

Goals may define:

* Target BPM
* Minimum quality
* Repertoire readiness
* Concert duration
* Competition preparation
* Exam preparation

---

## Study Plan

A structured collection of material assigned for a specific period.

A study plan may include:

* Exercises
* Variants
* Repertoire sections
* Goals

Study plans may be created by teachers or self-managed musicians.
