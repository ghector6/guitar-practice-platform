# Conceptual Model

## Purpose

The conceptual model defines the main entities, relationships, aggregate roots, and bounded contexts that compose the Guitar Practice Analytics Platform.

Its purpose is to provide a stable domain model that supports future implementations in Java, SQL databases, ETL pipelines, and Business Intelligence systems.

---

# Bounded Contexts

The system is divided into four major domains:

* Master Data
* Study Plan
* Practice Data
* Analytics

---

# Master Data Context

This context contains relatively static information that defines the educational environment.

## Entities

### User

Represents a person interacting with the platform.

Roles may include:

* Teacher
* Student
* Self-Managed Musician

---

### Institution

Educational organization containing teachers and students.

---

### Exercise

Technical material used to develop one or more technical abilities.

---

### Variant

Specific way of performing an exercise or repertoire section.

---

### Technique Element

Technical ability required or developed during practice.

Examples:

* Arpeggios
* Scales
* Slurs
* Independence
* Barre
* Position Shifts

---

### Piece

Musical work assigned for study.

---

### Section

Range of measures belonging to a piece.

Sections isolate technical difficulties and connect repertoire with exercises.

---

---

# Study Plan Context

Responsible for organizing the learning process.

## Entities

### Study Plan

Represents material assigned for a period of time.

---

### Goal

Represents measurable objectives.

Examples:

* BPM targets
* Quality thresholds
* Repertoire readiness
* Concert preparation

---

### Study Plan Exercise

Associative entity connecting exercises with a study plan.

May contain:

* Variant
* Target BPM
* Difficulty
* Priority

---

### Study Plan Section

Associative entity connecting repertoire sections with a study plan.

May contain:

* Target BPM
* Target Quality
* Priority

---

# Relationships

Study Plan → Goal

One-to-Many

---

Study Plan → Study Plan Exercise

One-to-Many

---

Study Plan → Study Plan Section

One-to-Many

---

# Aggregate Roots

Study Plan Aggregate Root:

* StudyPlan

---

# Practice Data Context

Responsible for preserving historical records.

Practice records are immutable.

## Entities

### Session

Represents a continuous period of practice.

Session types:

* Technique
* Repertoire
* Mixed

---

### Technical Practice

Represents execution of technical exercises.

Captures:

* BPM
* Repetitions
* Errors
* Subjective Score
* Quality

---

### Repertoire Practice

Represents execution of repertoire sections.

Captures:

* BPM
* Repetitions
* Errors
* Subjective Score
* Quality

---

# Relationships

Session → Technical Practice

One-to-Many

---

Session → Repertoire Practice

One-to-Many

---

# Aggregate Roots

Practice Aggregate Root:

* Session

---

# Analytics Context

Responsible for transforming historical records into actionable insights.

Analytics does not own operational data.

It consumes data from Practice Data and Study Plan contexts.

---

## Analytical Subjects

### Practice Quality

Measures effectiveness of practice.

---

### Tempo Progression

Tracks speed development.

---

### Goal Achievement

Evaluates objective completion.

---

### Repertoire Readiness

Measures concert preparation.

---

### Student Performance

Evaluates progress over time.

---

### Teacher Performance

Measures educational effectiveness.

---

# Aggregate Roots

Analytics does not own aggregates.

It consumes information generated by other contexts.

# Design Principles

The conceptual model follows:

* Domain-Driven Design (DDD)
* Clean Architecture
* Event-Oriented Modeling

The domain model should remain stable regardless of future technological changes.
